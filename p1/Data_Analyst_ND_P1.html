Statistics: The Science of Decisions Project Instructions¶

In a Stroop task, participants are presented with a list of words, with each word displayed in a color of ink. The participants task is to say out loud the color of the ink in which the word is printed. The task has two conditions: a congruent words condition, and an incongruent words condition. In the congruent words condition, the words being displayed are color words whose names match the colors in which they are printed: for example RED, BLUE. In the incongruent words condition, the words displayed are color words whose names do not match the colors in which they are printed: for example PURPLE, ORANGE. In each case, we measure the time it takes to name the ink colors in equallysized lists. Each participant will go through and record a time from each condition.

2A. What is an apappropriate set of hypotheses for this task?

Null hypothesis: $H_0: \mu_a - \mu_b = 0$

NO significant difference between average stroop test times in seconds for congruent and incongruent conditions.

Alternative hypothesis: $H_a: \mu_a - \mu_b \neq 0$

HAS significant difference between average stroop test times in seconds for congruent and incongruent conditions.

2B. What kind of statistical test do you expect to perform? Justify your choices.

Paired two sample t-test with the assumption: the data is normally distributed[1].

The reason for that is because:

  1. with this sample set, it has matched pair of similar independent variables
  2. the goal of the test is to compare whether there is a significant difference between the means of two related groups
